About Me

I am a systems-minded engineer working at the intersection of backend engineering, data infrastructure, and applied machine learning. Over the last several years I have designed, built, and operated production services, data platforms, and ML systems that go from raw signals to reliable product features. I enjoy turning complex research prototypes into robust, maintainable production software and I focus on measurable product impact and operational reliability.

Professional approach

I take a product-first engineering approach: I prefer simple, observable designs that make iteration and troubleshooting fast. I emphasize automated testing, CI/CD, monitoring, and modular architecture so models and services remain maintainable as they scale. My typical work blends software engineering discipline (APIs, service architecture, DB design) with ML best practices (reproducible pipelines, careful validation, and model deployment patterns).

Experience highlights

  • Built high-throughput backend systems for ad tracking and attribution at WiseTrack.
  • Conducted ML research in biological sequence modeling at Sharif University and combined deep feature extractors with ensemble predictors for interaction prediction.
  • Led a multi-person engineering team to design and ship a multi-vendor marketplace (AVLIsland).
  • Delivered production web platforms and training infrastructure for educational bootcamps.

Interests & focus areas

Distributed systems, reliable data pipelines, model serving at scale, ML for healthcare & bioinformatics, real-time analytics, and building engineering teams and practices that enable safe, repeatable delivery of ML-enabled features.
